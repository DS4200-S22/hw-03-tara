HTML+CSS & Marks+Channels

The data in the bubble and pie chart is quantitative and categorical attributes. The marks are the area or the angle of the slices of the pie chart. The location of the bubbles on the graph (points) are also used to display identities for the pies. The channels are color and location of the pies and color represents the change in attributes.

The data in the bump chart is quantitative and categorical. The dataset types are made up of trees and graphs which connect the points. The marks used in this visualization are lines and points The points show the items and the lines show the connections among them. The channels utilized are color and position which represent the different attributes and the change in attributes respectively.

The data in the mind map is categorical attributes. The types of the data and dataset are graphs and trees. The marks used are points and lines connecting the nodes. The lines connect the categories represented as nodes in the visualization. The channels are color, position, and length of the nodes.

The data in the stacked bar chart are categorical attributes. The marks used are lines composing the bars. The channels used are the height of the bar as well as the area and position of the subsections of the bar.
